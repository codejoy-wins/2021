Chess Lessons!

2064 Rating

I am a chess expert.

The computer doesn't like my rook sacrifice but it's probably my favorite move ever.

Funny enough, the computer actually recommends a4, sacrificing a pawn, and if knight takes then it recommends sacrificing the rook, because then the knight isn't able to defend the king.

So it's not that it didn't like to sacrifice, it's that I didn't sacrifice enough! Computers are savage. I knew the knight could defend but I thought I had such a nice position in that variation that he wouldn't be able to defend. Sure enough, he makes one mistake and then it's basically over. By the time he blunders the rook his position is completely lost. I had cut off all escape for his king and was ready to check him on multiple squares where he couldn't block, so the queen came out to try to stop that, leaving the rook hanging, and then everything fell apart.

I have worked for Educhess and Chess Wizards.

My rating of 2064 is on a scale from 100-3000. 2900 has never been reached by a person, but some machines are that strong.

2500 is required to become a Grandmaster. I guess that's my ultimate goal in chess.

My favorite players are Mikhail Tal and Bobby Fischer. Of the players who are alive I like Kasparov, Magnus, and Firouzja.

My favorite way to follow or learn chess is with the GothamChess YouTube Channel.
